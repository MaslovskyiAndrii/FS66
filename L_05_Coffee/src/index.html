The Best Coffee Regions

Coffee geography

Every region has its own character

Climate, altitude, rainfall and soil give coffee its aroma, body and taste notes. Explore six famous coffee regions and compare their unique profiles.

01 Arabica leader

Brazil

Arabica dominates both Brazil and the world as a whole with about 85% of production. In Brazil, arabica is grown mainly in coffee-growing states, while robusta is primarily grown in Espirito Santo.

Learn more about Brazil
02 Balanced taste

Colombia

Colombia is famous for mild, well-balanced coffee beans. Most coffee is grown in the Colombian coffee growing axis region and exported to many countries worldwide.

Learn more about Colombia
03 Monsoon coffee

India

Indian coffee is grown mostly in southern India under monsoon rainfall conditions. Arabica and Robusta are the two well-known species grown in the region.

Learn more about India
04 Chocolate notes

Ethiopia

Ethiopian beans include Longberry, Shortberry and Mocha. Mocha Harars are known for complex chocolate, spice and citrus notes.

Learn more about Ethiopia
05 High altitude

Costa Rica

Costa Rican coffee beans are considered among the best in the world. Tarrazu is known for desirable coffee beans, often grown at altitudes of 1200 to 1700 meters.

Learn more about Costa Rica
06 Intense flavor

Kenya

The acidic soil of central Kenya, sunlight and rainfall create excellent conditions. Kenyan coffee is known for intense flavor, full body and pleasant aroma.

Learn more about Kenya
